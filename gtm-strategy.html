GTM Strategy

You don't needa bigger budget

You need someone to decide who you're selling to, what channel earns them, and what you say once you're there, before another dollar goes into "more outbound, more ads."

Most pre-seed and seed teams don't have a GTM problem. They have a too many half-started motions problem: a bit of outbound, a bit of ads, a bit of content, none of it pointed at the same buyer.

What "GTM strategy" actually means here

Not a 40-page deck. A decision document you'll actually use.

Deliverable 01
ICP definition, sharpened

Company size, industry, buying triggers, and the two or three job titles who actually say yes, not a broad "B2B SaaS companies" target.

Deliverable 02
Channel priority, ranked

Where your specific ICP actually spends attention right now, ranked by effort-to-first-result, not every channel at once, diluted.

Deliverable 03
A 90-day motion, not a vision

What to run first, what to measure, and what "working" looks like at day 30, 60, and 90. Concrete enough to hand to whoever executes it.

How it gets built

Built from your actual sales data, not a generic framework.

01

Audit what's already working

Look at your best-fit customers so far and find the pattern. Most teams already have a signal, just buried in scattered wins.

02

Narrow the ICP

Cut the target down to the segment most likely to convert fastest, even if it feels smaller than you'd like at first.

03

Pick one primary channel

Outbound, ads, content, or partnerships, chosen based on where your ICP already pays attention, not what's trendy.

04

Hand off an execution plan

Specific enough that you, a hire, or I can start running it Monday morning, no strategy-to-execution gap left open.

Often paired with LinkedIn Outbound. A sharp GTM plan is only as good as the channel actually running it.
